Newly Diagnosed Action Plan

Breathe. You found this page, which means you are already fighting for your child. MLD is serious, but there is real hope — Lenmeldy gene therapy has shown 100% survival at age 6 for pre-symptomatic babies. You do not have to figure this out alone. The organizations below exist specifically to help families like yours.

This Week — The Critical First Calls

▼

These are the most time-sensitive steps. Start at the top and work your way down. You do not need to do them all in one day, but begin as soon as you can.

  1. Call Orchard Assist: 833-672-4278

    They are the manufacturer’s support program for Lenmeldy gene therapy. They assign a dedicated case manager who handles insurance investigation, prior authorization, financial assistance, and coordinates with treatment centers. This is call #1.

  2. Call the MLD Foundation: 503-656-4808

    They have been fighting MLD for 20+ years. They have a “What Do I Do Next?” guide, a video library, and will talk through your family’s specific situation. Email: deansuhr@MLDfoundation.org

  3. Email Cure MLD: info@curemld.com

    Ask for the Ambassador Program. They will connect you with a parent who has been through an MLD diagnosis — someone who understands what you are feeling and can walk alongside you. They also send care packages to newly diagnosed families.

  4. Confirm the genetic diagnosis

    Biallelic ARSA mutations must be confirmed via molecular genetic testing. Your geneticist can expedite this.

    Important: Approximately 7–15% of people carry a “pseudodeficiency” allele that causes low enzyme levels but does NOT cause MLD. Genetic sequencing distinguishes this from disease-causing mutations.
  5. Contact a treatment center directly

    Do not wait for referral chains. Call your nearest Qualified Treatment Center directly. There are five centers in the U.S.: Minneapolis, Atlanta, Philadelphia, Houston, and San Francisco.

This Month — Building Your Support System

▼

Once the critical first calls are made, use this month to build the framework your family will rely on through treatment and beyond.

  1. Call Global Genes RARE Concierge: 949-248-7273

    Free one-on-one navigator assigned to your family. They run a 6-week structured program for newly diagnosed families with follow-up at 3, 6, and 12 months. Website: globalgenes.org

  2. Call NMDP Patient Navigators: 888-999-6743

    Free support through every stage of the gene therapy journey. Text or call. Email: patientinfo@nmdp.org

  3. Contact Ronald McDonald House near your treatment center

    Waitlists can be long — start early. Housing is free or up to $25/night. See our Treatment Centers page for specific locations and nearby Ronald McDonald Houses.

  4. Arrange travel (if your treatment center is not local)
    • Miracle Flights: 800-359-1711 — free commercial flights for child + 2 parents
    • Angel Flight: free volunteer pilot flights (angelflightwest.org and regional sites)
  5. Talk to your hospital social worker

    They can initiate applications for financial assistance programs that require a provider referral.

  6. Test siblings

    Each sibling has a 25% chance of being affected. This is time-sensitive — a pre-symptomatic sibling could also qualify for gene therapy. See our Siblings & Family Testing page for details.

Financial Help — You Are Not Alone

▼

MLD treatment is expensive, but there are organizations dedicated to helping families with both medical and non-medical costs. Do not let financial fear stop you from pursuing treatment.

Orchard Assist

833-672-4278

Insurance navigation, financial assistance assessment, and treatment access coordination for Lenmeldy gene therapy.

Patient Advocate Foundation

800-532-5274

Fights insurance denials and medical debt. $174M in assistance since 2018. Website: patientadvocate.org

NORD Emergency Relief

860-556-2208

Non-medical costs: rent, utilities, car repairs. Email: paprogram@rarediseases.org

Fred’s Footsteps

484-368-3602

Up to $20K/year for non-medical expenses. CHOP area but worth asking. Website: fredsfootsteps.org

Cure MLD Travel Grants

$500 local, $1,000 international per affected child. Contact: info@curemld.com

UnitedHealthcare Children’s Foundation

Up to $5,000/grant for medical services. Child must be 16 or under. Website: uhccf.org

FMLA

12 weeks unpaid, job-protected leave for caring for a child with a serious health condition. Employer must have 50+ employees.

Modest Needs

Self-Sufficiency Grants, average ~$180, max $1,000. Website: modestneeds.org

211 (United Way)

Call 211 or visit 211.org for local food, housing, health, and crisis support.

Emotional Support — Taking Care of Yourself

▼

A rare disease diagnosis does not just affect your child — it affects you. These organizations provide support specifically for families navigating the emotional weight of a serious diagnosis.

  • Give an Hour

    Free mental health counseling from licensed therapists, specifically for rare disease caregivers. Peer support groups every 2 weeks. Website: giveanhour.org/rare

  • Center for Chronic Illness

    Free virtual support groups with licensed mental health professionals. Website: thecenterforchronicillness.org

  • Cure MLD Newly Diagnosed Support Group

    Private Facebook group for families in the first 2–3 years post-diagnosis. Contact info@curemld.com to join.

  • ULF Monthly Support Groups

    Online caregiver support for leukodystrophy families. Phone: 800-728-5483. Website: ulf.org

  • Caregiver Action Network

    Resources and community for rare disease caregivers. Website: caregiveraction.org/rarecaregivers

You do not have to be in crisis to ask for help. Many of these resources are designed for families who are coping but struggling — not just families in emergency. Reaching out early is one of the best things you can do for yourself and your child. See our full Emotional & Mental Health page for more.
