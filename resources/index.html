Organizations & Support

No family should navigate MLD alone. These organizations provide medical guidance, family connections, financial assistance, and advocacy. Each one brings something different to the table — and many families find that connecting with multiple organizations gives them the most complete support.

▼ MLD Foundation

Website: mld.foundation  |  Established: 1999

The MLD Foundation is the premier family resource dedicated specifically to Metachromatic Leukodystrophy. For over 25 years, they have been the central hub connecting MLD families, researchers, and clinicians worldwide.

What They Offer

  • Annual MLD Family Conference — The largest gathering of MLD families, researchers, and clinicians. The 2025 conference was held in Atlanta, GA. These conferences are a powerful way to connect with other families and hear directly from leading researchers.
  • Extensive Video Library — Educational videos covering MLD science, treatment options, family stories, and conference presentations.
  • Advocacy for Newborn Screening — The MLD Foundation has been a driving force behind getting MLD added to newborn screening panels nationwide.
  • MLD Doctors Directory — A curated list of physicians with MLD expertise, searchable by location and specialty.
  • MLD Map — A geographic tool for locating MLD-related services, specialists, and treatment centers in your area.
  • "Get Connected" Program — Family-to-family support that pairs you with another MLD family who understands what you are going through.
▼ Cure MLD

Website: curemld.com  |  Contact: info@curemld.com

Cure MLD provides hands-on, practical support for MLD families — especially those who are newly diagnosed. Their programs are designed to meet you where you are and connect you with the resources you need right away.

Ambassador Program

Newly diagnosed families are paired with experienced MLD families who have walked this road. An outreach coordinator contacts you directly to make the match. This is one of the most valued programs in the MLD community.

Financial Support

  • Travel Grants: $500 for local travel; $1,000 per affected child for international travel to treatment or conferences.
  • Emergency Grants: $500 or more based on documented need for families facing unexpected financial hardship.

Newly Diagnosed Care Packages

Cure MLD sends care packages to newly diagnosed families that include books and a medical resource guide to help you understand MLD and your options.

Free Books

  • Living with Leukodystrophy — A comprehensive guide for families.
  • Loie's Disease — A children's book that helps explain leukodystrophy to young siblings and family members.
  • Fixing Genes, Treating Disease — A children's book about gene therapy, helpful for explaining treatment to kids.

Additional Support

  • Medicaid navigation and IEP advocacy assistance
  • Clinical trial guidance and information
▼ United Leukodystrophy Foundation (ULF)

Website: ulf.org  |  Established: 1982

The United Leukodystrophy Foundation is one of the oldest organizations supporting families affected by all forms of leukodystrophy. While not MLD-specific, their broad scope means extensive resources and a large support network.

  • Research Funding — ULF funds scientific research into all leukodystrophies, including MLD.
  • Disease Information & Medical Referrals — Comprehensive information about leukodystrophies and referrals to knowledgeable specialists.
  • Support Network — Connects families affected by all leukodystrophies for peer support and shared experience.
  • Family Programming — Events and programs designed for affected individuals and their families.
▼ Hunter's Hope Foundation

Website: huntershope.org

Founded by NFL quarterback Jim Kelly, whose son Hunter was diagnosed with Krabbe disease (another leukodystrophy). Hunter's Hope has become a major force in leukodystrophy care infrastructure across the United States.

Leukodystrophy Care Network (LCN)

The LCN is a network of 16 certified care centers plus 2 affiliate/candidate centers across the US. Each center has dedicated care coordinators who help families navigate the medical system and ensure comprehensive, coordinated care. This network is one of the most important practical resources for leukodystrophy families.

Other Programs

  • Education & Awareness — Programs to increase understanding of leukodystrophies among healthcare providers and the public.
  • Data Registry — A registry that helps track outcomes and advance research.
  • Family Support Programs — Direct support for affected families including events and resources.
▼ NORD (National Organization for Rare Disorders)

Website: rarediseases.org

NORD is the leading umbrella organization for rare diseases in the United States. While not MLD-specific, they provide critical programs that benefit all rare disease families.

  • MedicAlert Program — Subsidized medical alert IDs for rare disease patients, ensuring emergency responders have critical medical information.
  • Caregiver Respite Support — Programs to provide temporary relief for primary caregivers.
  • Rare Disease Patient Assistance Programs — Help with medication costs, insurance navigation, and accessing treatments.
  • Policy Advocacy — NORD advocates at the federal and state level for policies that benefit rare disease patients, including orphan drug legislation and newborn screening expansion.
▼ Global Genes

Website: globalgenes.org

Global Genes is a leading rare disease advocacy organization that empowers patients and families through education, community, and support.

  • Rare Disease Advocacy — Training and tools for families who want to become advocates for their child and their community.
  • Family Support — Resources and connections for rare disease families.
  • RARE Foundation Alliance — A network of rare disease organizations working together to advance shared goals.
▼ Bethany's Hope Foundation

A Canadian charity dedicated to funding MLD research. Bethany's Hope supports scientific efforts to find better treatments and ultimately a cure for MLD, with a focus on supporting research projects that have direct potential to help affected families.

▼ National Tay-Sachs & Allied Diseases Association (NTSAD)

NTSAD supports families affected by lysosomal storage diseases, including MLD. While their primary focus is Tay-Sachs and related conditions, MLD families are welcomed as part of their broader community.

  • Annual Conference — Brings together families affected by multiple lysosomal storage diseases for education, support, and connection.
  • Peer Support — Connects families facing similar challenges across the lysosomal disease spectrum.
Where to start: Begin with Cure MLD's Ambassador Program — being connected with a family who has been through this is often the most valuable support in the early days. Having someone who understands MLD firsthand, who can answer your questions and share what they've learned, makes an enormous difference.
