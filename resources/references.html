References & Further Reading

This page collects the key medical references, clinical guidelines, treatment resources, and research publications related to MLD. These are the sources behind the information presented throughout this site, and they can serve as starting points for deeper research and conversations with your medical team.

▼ Medical References
  • GeneReviews: Arylsulfatase A Deficiency — Comprehensive, expert-authored review of MLD genetics, diagnosis, and management. One of the most authoritative medical resources available.
    ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK1130
  • NORD: Metachromatic Leukodystrophy — Patient-friendly overview from the National Organization for Rare Disorders, covering symptoms, causes, diagnosis, and treatment.
    rarediseases.org
  • NIH MedlinePlus Genetics: Metachromatic Leukodystrophy — Clear, accessible explanation of MLD from the National Institutes of Health, including genetics and inheritance.
    medlineplus.gov
  • StatPearls: Metachromatic Leukodystrophy — Clinical reference covering pathophysiology, evaluation, and management. Frequently updated.
    ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK560744
  • Cleveland Clinic: Metachromatic Leukodystrophy — Patient education resource covering symptoms, diagnosis, and treatment options.
    clevelandclinic.org
▼ Clinical Guidelines
  • Consensus Guidelines for MLD Monitoring and Management in the U.S. (2024) — Expert consensus on how to monitor and manage MLD patients, including recommended assessments, monitoring schedules, and care coordination. Essential reading for understanding what comprehensive MLD care looks like.
    PubMed Central
▼ Treatment Resources
  • Lenmeldy Official Site — Official information about Lenmeldy (atidarsagene autotemcel) gene therapy, including mechanism of action, clinical trial results, and patient eligibility.
    lenmeldy.com
  • Lenmeldy Support / Orchard Assist — Patient support program from Orchard Therapeutics, providing financial assistance, insurance navigation, and treatment coordination for Lenmeldy patients.
    lenmeldy.com/support
  • Orchard Therapeutics FDA Approval Announcement — Official announcement of FDA approval of Lenmeldy for MLD, including clinical data and regulatory details.
    orchardtx.com
  • Boston Children's Hospital Gene Therapy Program — One of the leading gene therapy programs for leukodystrophies, including MLD research and treatment.
    childrenshospital.org
  • Lenmeldy Treatment Centers — List of qualified treatment centers authorized to administer Lenmeldy gene therapy.
    lenmeldy.com
▼ Research Publications
  • The Lancet: Lentiviral Hematopoietic Stem Cell Gene Therapy for MLD — Long-Term Results — Landmark publication reporting long-term outcomes of gene therapy for MLD, demonstrating sustained ARSA enzyme production and clinical benefit in pre-symptomatic and early symptomatic patients.
    thelancet.com
  • Nature: FDA Approves Gene Therapy for Metachromatic Leukodystrophy — Coverage of the FDA's approval of Lenmeldy, the first gene therapy for MLD, including scientific context and implications for other genetic diseases.
    nature.com
  • CHOP: Improved Gene Therapy Approach (EA1 Vector) — Research from Children's Hospital of Philadelphia on an improved lentiviral vector (EA1) that may deliver higher ARSA enzyme levels and better outcomes than current gene therapy approaches.
    chop.edu
  • Long-Term Outcomes After Allogeneic HSCT for MLD — Study examining long-term results of stem cell transplant for MLD, providing evidence on which patients benefit most and expected outcomes.
    PubMed Central
  • MLD: New Therapy Advancements and Emerging Research — Review of the current research landscape for MLD, including next-generation gene therapy, substrate reduction therapy, and other experimental approaches.
    PubMed Central
▼ Newborn Screening
  • HHS.gov: MLD Added to the RUSP (December 2025) — Official federal announcement of MLD's addition to the Recommended Uniform Screening Panel, including the evidence review and advisory committee recommendation.
    hhs.gov
  • New York State: First State to Implement MLD Newborn Screening — Information about New York's implementation of MLD screening, which began in November 2025, making it the first state in the nation to screen for MLD.
    health.ny.gov
  • Federal Register: RUSP Addition Documentation — Official federal register documentation of the RUSP addition process and evidence review for MLD.
    federalregister.gov
  • MLD Newborn Screening Resource — Dedicated resource tracking state-by-state newborn screening implementation, advocacy tools, and educational materials.
    mldnewbornscreening.org

▼ Clinical Trials
  • ClinicalTrials.gov: MLD Studies — Search the US federal database of clinical trials for all active and recruiting MLD studies. This is the most comprehensive and up-to-date source for finding clinical trials.
    clinicaltrials.gov
About clinical trials: New trials open (and close) regularly. If you are interested in clinical trial participation for your child, check ClinicalTrials.gov periodically and discuss options with your child's neurologist or geneticist. Organizations like Cure MLD can also help you navigate the clinical trial landscape.
