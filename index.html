⚠ Just Diagnosed? Do This Today

Time is the most critical factor in MLD. Treatment outcomes are dramatically better when started before symptoms appear. If your child is pre-symptomatic, the window is open — but every week matters.

  1. Call Orchard Assist today: 833-672-4278 (833-ORCHAST) — they navigate insurance, access, and treatment logistics for Lenmeldy gene therapy
  2. Get a referral to one of the 5 Qualified Treatment Centers (Minneapolis, Atlanta, Philadelphia, Houston, or San Francisco)
  3. Test all siblings immediately — each sibling has a 25% chance of being affected. Learn more
  4. Connect with Cure MLD for their newly diagnosed family ambassador program and care package
  5. Request genetic counseling to identify exact mutations and plan for the future. Details here

There Is Hope: Gene Therapy Changes Everything

In March 2024, the FDA approved Lenmeldy (atidarsagene autotemcel) — the first gene therapy for MLD. For pre-symptomatic babies with late infantile MLD, the outcomes are remarkable:

100% Alive at age 6
(vs 58% untreated)
85% Normal cognition
and language
71% Walking without
help at age 5

Source: Orchard Therapeutics clinical data, 37 children, median 6.7 years follow-up. Read more about Lenmeldy →

💊 Treatment Window

  • Lenmeldy works best before symptoms appear
  • Late infantile symptoms start between 6–30 months
  • Treatment process takes 9–12 months
  • 5 treatment centers in the US
  • FDA approved for children age 6 and under

📊 Key Facts

  • Affects 1 in 40,000–160,000 people
  • Caused by mutations in the ARSA gene
  • Autosomal recessive inheritance (both parents are carriers)
  • Late infantile is most common type (50–60% of cases)
  • Added to national newborn screening panel December 2025

About This Resource

This site was created to help families navigate the overwhelming first days and weeks after an MLD diagnosis. It gathers information from the NIH, NORD, GeneReviews, the MLD Foundation, Cure MLD, clinical trial databases, and published medical literature into one organized, searchable resource.

MLD is rare. Finding clear, comprehensive, parent-friendly information shouldn’t be. Everything here is meant to support — never replace — your child’s medical team.

If this resource helped your family, consider sharing it with your child’s care team so they can share it with newly diagnosed families.
