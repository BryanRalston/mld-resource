Qualified Treatment Centers

Lenmeldy gene therapy can only be administered at treatment centers that have been specifically qualified by Orchard Therapeutics and the FDA. These centers have undergone training in the treatment protocol, have the specialized equipment and expertise needed, and have been approved to handle every phase of the gene therapy process.

As of early 2026, there are five qualified treatment centers in the United States.

FDA-Qualified Treatment Centers

▼

1. M Health Fairview / University of Minnesota Masonic Children’s Hospital

  • Location: Minneapolis, Minnesota
  • Overview: The University of Minnesota has one of the longest histories in stem cell transplantation for metabolic diseases, dating back to pioneering work in the 1990s. Their leukodystrophy program has treated more inherited metabolic diseases with transplant than nearly any other center in the world.
  • Strengths: Deep institutional expertise in leukodystrophies specifically; extensive experience with both HSCT and gene therapy for MLD; well-established family support services

2. Children’s Healthcare of Atlanta (CHOA)

  • Location: Atlanta, Georgia
  • Overview: CHOA is one of the largest pediatric healthcare systems in the country, with a robust bone marrow transplant and cellular therapy program. Their team includes specialists in inherited metabolic diseases.
  • Strengths: Large pediatric system with comprehensive support services; located in a major transportation hub with good flight access from the Southeast

3. Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia (CHOP)

  • Location: Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
  • Overview: CHOP is consistently ranked among the top children’s hospitals in the nation. Their leukodystrophy center is one of the most comprehensive in the world, combining clinical care with active research. CHOP’s team is also developing next-generation gene therapy vectors for MLD.
  • Strengths: World-class leukodystrophy research program; strong pipeline for next-gen treatments; experienced in the full spectrum of MLD management

4. Texas Children’s Hospital / Baylor College of Medicine

  • Location: Houston, Texas
  • Overview: Texas Children’s is the largest children’s hospital in the United States. Their Center for Cell and Gene Therapy, a collaboration with Baylor College of Medicine, has extensive experience in gene therapy and transplant for genetic diseases.
  • Strengths: Massive institutional resources; strong gene therapy research program; Texas Medical Center provides access to virtually every specialty

5. UCSF Benioff Children’s Hospital

  • Location: San Francisco, California
  • Overview: UCSF Benioff Children’s Hospital is a leading pediatric medical center on the West Coast with an established program in stem cell transplantation and gene therapy for inherited diseases.
  • Strengths: Strong research program; West Coast location providing access for families in the western United States, Hawaii, and the Pacific
Emerging center: Boston Children’s Hospital has a dedicated Gene Therapy Program that is actively evaluating MLD patients. While not yet officially listed as a Qualified Treatment Center, they may be in the qualification process. Contact their Gene Therapy Program at 617-919-1992 or gene.therapy@childrens.harvard.edu to inquire about current status and MLD services.

Housing Near Each Center

▼

Every qualified treatment center city has a Ronald McDonald House nearby. These provide free or very low-cost housing specifically for families of children receiving medical treatment. Rooms are limited, so apply as early as possible — your treatment center social worker can help coordinate this.

You are never turned away from Ronald McDonald House for inability to pay. Most locations charge nothing or a nominal fee up to $25/night, but payment is never required if your family cannot afford it.

1. Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia (CHOP)

Ronald McDonald House Philadelphia — the original Ronald McDonald House, founded in 1974.

  • Capacity: 127 rooms across two locations
  • In-hospital rooms: Family rooms available inside CHOP on the oncology unit and at Seashore House
  • Cost: Free or up to $25/night (never required if you cannot pay)
  • Website: rmhcphilly.org

2. University of Minnesota Fairview (Minneapolis)

Minneapolis has two Ronald McDonald House locations:

  • Ronald McDonald House Oak Street — 48 rooms and suites. Includes a K-12 school, gym, and playgrounds. Address: 621 Oak Street SE, Minneapolis. Phone: 612-805-3467
  • Ronald McDonald House In-Hospital (Chicago Ave) — located inside Children’s Minneapolis, 3rd floor. Address: 2525 Chicago Ave S, Minneapolis. Phone: 612-874-4800

3. Texas Children’s Hospital (Houston)

Houston has both on-campus and nearby housing:

  • Ronald McDonald House at Texas Children’s — 20 rooms, 4th floor Abercrombie Building (on campus). Phone: 832-824-8600
  • Ronald McDonald House Holcombe (flagship) — larger facility nearby. Phone: 713-795-3500. Website: rmhchouston.org

4. Children’s Healthcare of Atlanta (CHOA)

Ronald McDonald House Atlanta — a newly built facility located directly on the CHOA campus. This is the first Ronald McDonald House in the Southeast built on a hospital campus.

  • Capacity: 110 guest rooms
  • Amenities: Gaming room, craft areas, meditation spaces
  • Phone: 404-315-1133
  • Website: ronaldmcdonaldhouseatl.org

5. UCSF Benioff Children’s Hospital (San Francisco)

Ronald McDonald House Bay Area — located inside UCSF.

  • Capacity: 11 rooms inside the hospital, plus a Family Room on the 6th floor
  • Meals: Free lunch and dinner provided
  • Eligibility: Families must live 50+ miles from the hospital
  • Contact: America Martinez, 415-502-9868
  • Website: rmhcbayarea.org
Apply early. Ronald McDonald House rooms fill up. Ask your treatment center social worker to submit your application as soon as your treatment dates are confirmed. Many locations also have waiting lists, so the sooner you apply, the better your chances.

Getting There: Travel Assistance Programs

▼

If your family needs to travel a significant distance to reach a qualified treatment center, several organizations provide free flights for children with serious medical conditions and their families. These are separate from insurance-based travel benefits and can be combined with other support.

Miracle Flights

Free commercial airfare for children 17 and under, plus up to 2 parents or guardians.

  • Phone: 800-359-1711
  • Website: miracleflights.org
  • Covers flights on major commercial airlines at no cost to the family
  • Apply well in advance of your treatment dates

Angel Flight

Free flights on volunteer private aircraft. Angel Flight operates through regional organizations that together cover the entire United States:

  • Angel Flight West: angelflightwest.org (western states)
  • Angel Flight Central: angelflightcentral.org (central states)
  • Angel Flight Northeast: angelflightne.org (northeastern states)
  • Angel Flight Southeast: angelflightse.org (southeastern states)

AeroAngel

Free private jet flights specifically for critically ill children.

  • Website: aeroangel.org
  • Designed for children who need to travel longer distances or who have medical conditions that make commercial flights difficult
Combine resources. These flight programs can be used alongside Cure MLD travel grants, Orchard Assist support, and Ronald McDonald House housing. Your treatment center social worker can help you layer multiple programs to minimize out-of-pocket costs.

What “Qualified” Means

▼

Not every hospital that performs stem cell transplants can administer Lenmeldy. The “qualified” designation means the center has:

  • Completed specialized training in the Lenmeldy treatment protocol, including stem cell collection, conditioning, infusion, and monitoring
  • Demonstrated the necessary infrastructure — apheresis capabilities, inpatient isolation rooms, pediatric intensive care, and the laboratory and pharmacy services needed to handle a gene therapy product
  • Been approved by Orchard Therapeutics and the FDA as meeting all regulatory requirements for administering this specific therapy
  • Established protocols for patient selection, pre-treatment evaluation, post-treatment monitoring, and long-term follow-up

This designation is specific to Lenmeldy. A hospital may be an excellent transplant center but not be qualified for Lenmeldy if they have not completed the qualification process.

How to Get Referred

▼

There are two main pathways to connect with a qualified treatment center:

Through Orchard Assist

Orchard Assist (833-672-4278) is designed to be the single point of contact for families seeking Lenmeldy. They can:

  • Confirm your child’s preliminary eligibility based on diagnosis and age
  • Connect you with the most appropriate qualified treatment center
  • Help coordinate the referral process
  • Begin the insurance and access process in parallel

Through Physician Referral

Your child’s geneticist, neurologist, or metabolic disease specialist can refer directly to any of the five qualified centers. Many families are first diagnosed at a local children’s hospital and then referred to one of the qualified centers for treatment. Your referring physician can coordinate with the treatment center’s intake team.

Orchard Assist: 833-672-4278 (833-ORCHAST). This is a free service. You do not need a physician referral to call — families can reach out directly. They can answer questions even if you are still in the process of getting a confirmed diagnosis.

Choosing a Center

▼

All five centers are qualified to administer Lenmeldy, but there are practical factors that may influence your choice:

Proximity

Treatment requires two separate hospitalizations and ongoing outpatient follow-up. Being closer to the center reduces travel burden, makes it easier for family members to visit, and simplifies follow-up appointments. However, do not let distance alone be the deciding factor — the right center for your child’s specific situation may outweigh the convenience of proximity.

Family Housing and Support

Ask each center about family housing options. Most have relationships with Ronald McDonald House or other family accommodation programs nearby. Some centers have more comprehensive family support services (social workers, family liaisons, sibling programs) than others.

Insurance Network

Verify that the center is in your insurance plan’s network, or that your plan will cover out-of-network care for this specialized treatment. Orchard Assist can help you navigate this. Out-of-network care may still be approved given the specialized nature of the therapy.

Center Experience

While all five centers are qualified, some may have treated more Lenmeldy patients than others. It is reasonable to ask how many patients the center has treated and about their experience with the specific protocol. Academic centers with active MLD research programs may also offer access to complementary clinical trials.

Questions to Ask the Treatment Center

  • How many Lenmeldy patients has your center treated?
  • What family housing and support services are available?
  • What is the typical timeline from initial visit to treatment?
  • Who will be on the care team (transplant physician, neurologist, genetic counselor, social worker)?
  • What is the plan for long-term follow-up?
  • Do you have experience managing complications specific to this therapy?
  • Are there related clinical trials I should know about?

Travel and Financial Support

▼

If the nearest qualified center is far from home, the financial and logistical burden of travel can be significant. Several resources may help:

Orchard Assist

Orchard Assist can help clarify what travel and lodging costs may be covered under your insurance plan or through manufacturer support programs. Some insurance plans cover travel for specialized treatments that are not available locally.

Cure MLD Travel Grants

Cure MLD offers travel grants of $500–$1,000 to help families cover travel expenses related to MLD treatment. These grants can help with airfare, gas, hotel stays, and meals during treatment-related travel.

Ronald McDonald House

All five treatment center cities have Ronald McDonald House locations that provide free or very low-cost housing for families of children receiving medical treatment. Ask your treatment center to help you apply early — availability can be limited.

Additional Resources

  • National Patient Travel Center (NPTC): Helps coordinate charitable medical transportation
  • Angel Flight America: Network of volunteer pilots who provide free air transportation for medical treatment
  • Corporate Angel Network: Arranges free flights for patients using empty seats on corporate jets
  • Hospital social workers: Can connect you with local and national resources specific to your situation

See our Grants & Charitable Assistance page for more financial support resources.

Leukodystrophy Care Network

▼

Separate from the five Lenmeldy-qualified treatment centers, the Hunter’s Hope Leukodystrophy Care Network includes 16 certified care centers across the United States. These are not gene therapy treatment centers — they are specialized centers for the ongoing management and care of children with leukodystrophies, including MLD.

These centers provide:

  • Multidisciplinary care teams experienced in leukodystrophies
  • Neurological monitoring and developmental assessments
  • Physical, occupational, and speech therapy coordination
  • Palliative care expertise when needed
  • Connection to the broader leukodystrophy community

If you live far from one of the five Lenmeldy treatment centers, a Hunter’s Hope Care Network center may be a valuable resource for your child’s ongoing care between treatment center visits. Your child’s treatment team can collaborate with a local care network center to provide coordinated follow-up.

Treatment center vs. care center: Your child may receive gene therapy (or HSCT) at one of the five qualified treatment centers, and then receive ongoing management at a Hunter’s Hope Leukodystrophy care center closer to home. These two types of centers serve different but complementary roles.
